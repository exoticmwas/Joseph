Bat-eared Fox

The bat-eared fox is a small fox species found in Kenya and other parts of Africa. It belongs to the dog family and is easily recognized by its large ears.

This fox mainly feeds on insects such as termites, has six more teeth than other fox species, and can live alone or in small groups.

Bat-eared foxes inhabit savannahs, arid scrublands, and grassy plains. They are commonly found in Kenya, Tanzania, South Africa, Angola, and Zimbabwe.

The IUCN lists the bat-eared fox as "least concern" with a stable population. However, they face threats from hunters, diseases, and drought.
