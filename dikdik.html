Dikdik

The dik-dik is a type of dwarf antelope that lives in arid regions of East Africa. They can survive in dry environments because they obtain water from the vegetation they eat. To protect themselves from predators such as eagles and wild cats, they take cover in dense undergrowth.

There are four species of dik-diks, but only two are found in Kenya: Guenther’s dik-dik and Kirk’s dik-dik. Kirk’s dik-dik is the largest of the species, though still small compared to other antelopes. It stands just 45 centimeters (18 inches) tall and weighs only 7.2 kilograms (15.8 pounds). Guenther’s dik-dik is smaller but has a longer snout. The IUCN lists both Kirk’s dik-dik and Guenther’s dik-dik as least concern, though they are threatened by expanding agricultural settlements and excessive hunting in some areas.
